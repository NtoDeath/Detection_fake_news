# Test Texts for Fake News Detection CLI

Use these texts to test the fake news detector. Copy and paste them when prompted.

**Note:** Texts have been made STEREOTYPICAL to match Style model feature patterns:
- **FAKE:** High punctuation (!?), UPPERCASE words, emotional pronouns, charged adjectives, short sentences
- **REAL:** Low punctuation, neutral tone, academic style, factual adjectives, longer sentences

### Long Real 1
```
The human immune system is a complex biological defense mechanism that protects the body against pathogens including bacteria, viruses, fungi, and parasites. It includes physical barriers such as skin and mucous membranes, as well as specialized cells like white blood cells, lymphocytes, and macrophages. When a pathogen invades, the innate immune system provides a rapid initial response to contain the threat. The adaptive immune system then develops targeted responses by producing antibodies and activating T cells specific to the invading pathogen. Vaccines work by introducing a weakened or inactivated pathogen, allowing the immune system to develop antibodies and memory cells without causing severe illness. This creates immunity so that future exposure to the actual pathogen triggers a rapid response. Extensive clinical trials and ongoing monitoring have demonstrated that vaccines are safe and effective at reducing disease burden globally.
```

### Long Real 2
```
The process of photosynthesis in plants occurs primarily in the leaves, specifically within structures called chloroplasts. These organelles contain chlorophyll, a pigment that absorbs light energy at different wavelengths. When light energy is absorbed, water molecules are split into hydrogen and oxygen through a process called photolysis. The oxygen is released as waste, while the hydrogen powers the Calvin cycle, a series of reactions that fixes carbon dioxide into glucose. This glucose serves as an energy source and building block for plant growth. The overall reaction can be described as: carbon dioxide plus water, with light energy, produces glucose and oxygen. Different plants have adapted variations of this process, including C3 photosynthesis, C4 photosynthesis, and CAM photosynthesis, depending on their environmental conditions.
```

A major breakthrough came in 1974 when researchers Vint Cerf and Bob Kahn developed the TCP/IP protocol, which became the foundational standard for the Internet. In 1989, Tim Berners-Lee, a British physicist working at CERN in Switzerland, invented the World Wide Web and the HTTP protocol. This allowed documents to be linked through hypertext, creating what we know today as websites and web pages. The commercialization of the Internet began in 1991, and by the 1990s, services like America Online brought the Internet into homes worldwide. Today, the Internet connects billions of devices globally and serves as essential infrastructure for communication, commerce, education, and entertainment.

## Test Strategy

1. **Start with short texts** to observe model discrimination on stereotypical features
2. **Test medium texts** to see how fake style (high punctuation, emotion) vs real style (neutral tone) affects predictions
3. **Try long texts** to evaluate performance on sustained stylistic patterns
4. **Use mixed texts** for ambiguous content with conflicting signals

**Expected patterns:**
- FAKE texts should score HIGH on STYLE (due to aggressive punctuation, emotion, pronouns)
- REAL texts should score LOW on STYLE (due to neutral, academic tone)
- KNOWLEDGE will likely be unreliable (as discussed in EVIDENCE_RETRIEVAL_LIMITATIONS.md)
- FUSION combines both signals
